About Me

I am Chalonreay Kahindi, a trailblazing fashion designer, environmental activist, and tech innovator committed to transforming communities through sustainability, digital solutions, and advocacy. With a degree in Fashion Design and Marketing from Kenyatta University, I specialize in ethical fashion, upcycling, and circular design principles to combat textile waste and promote sustainable livelihoods. As a software development scholar with the Power Learn Project, I merge technology with sustainability, developing digital solutions for climate action and community empowerment. My leadership extends into advocacy, where I have played a pivotal role in mental health awareness, girl empowerment, and climate justice. I have impacted over 15,000 girls through menstrual health education, mentorship, and scholarships under PAMOJA Girls. Additionally, I spearheaded the Climate Voices & Action Hub, an initiative amplifying grassroots climate activists while fostering cross-sector collaborations for environmental justice.

Projects

Sustainable Fashion

As a regional coordinator with Trashion Kenya, I lead nationwide campaigns on upcycling, waste reduction, and sustainable fashion education. My expertise in zero-waste design and ethical production has influenced policy discussions and youth training programs. I run sewing classes for young mothers, equipping them with sustainable fashion skills that generate income while reducing landfill waste. My collaboration with the Jilore Community Forest Association integrates sustainable textiles with reforestation efforts, advocating for climate-resilient practices in the fashion industry. I have hosted workshops on eco-conscious design, advocating for responsible consumerism and the role of Indigenous knowledge in sustainable fashion. These initiatives empower youth to merge creativity with environmental responsibility.

Tech Innovations

With a deep interest in AI and sustainability, I develop Python-based applications that integrate climate data analytics and digital advocacy tools. My projects include creating Tkinter-based apps for environmental education and open-source contributions aimed at increasing accessibility to green technology. I actively engage in coding for climate action, working on data-driven solutions that enhance environmental awareness and resource efficiency. My GitHub repositories include projects focused on sustainable development, and I am currently exploring machine learning applications for climate resilience.

Advocacy & Leadership

As a leader within the Kenya Girl Guides Association, I champion programs that foster leadership, climate action, and gender equity among young girls. Through my mentorship, thousands of girls have been empowered to take active roles in conservation and advocacy. My role in PAMOJA Girls has led to the successful distribution of over 15,000 sanitary kits, full scholarships for 370 students, and the reintegration of 35 children with their families through holistic support programs. At the Climate Justice Camp in Tanzania, I contributed to discussions on youth-led climate action, Indigenous land rights, and policy advocacy, bringing a grassroots perspective to international forums. The Climate Voices & Action Hub, which I founded, serves as a platform for amplifying climate narratives from underrepresented communities, connecting activists, policymakers, and innovators in the fight against climate change.
